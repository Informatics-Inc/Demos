Our History

A Legacy of Dedication

A group of concerned citizens recognize the need for domestic violence assistance in their community. They create the Domestic Violence Aid Center.

1982
Establishing Assistance

The agency, now called Family Crisis Centers, goes from serving 4 counties to serving 17 in northwest Iowa. Increased human trafficking prompts us to develop a service to assisting these victims.

1984
Addressing Human Trafficking

Again, the need for assistance drives us to expand services. We establish the WISH Unit, a program dedicated to working with survivors of homicide and other violent crimes in 29 Iowa counties.

2013
Support for Survivors

The Iowa Attorney General establishes the Iowa Victim Service Call Center for victims and survivors of abuse and crime. The FCC begins incorporating this resource, which serves all of Iowa.

2015
Iowa Adds a Hotline

The FCC forms the Integrated Advocacy Services to offer support and advocacy within healthcare. The program serves 17 Iowa counties at 3 separate medical clinics.

2018
Entering Clinics
