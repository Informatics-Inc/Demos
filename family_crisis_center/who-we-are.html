Our History

A Legacy of Dedication

A group of concerned citizens recognize the need for domestic violence resources in the community. They created the Domestic Violence Aid Center, now known as Family Crisis Centers.

1982

Permanent shelter with office and counseling space opened.

2001

Opened a new facility with on-site housing to accommodate growing victim numbers and needs.

2009

We expanded our domestic violence service area from 4 counties to 17 northwest Iowa counties.

2013

Added a homicide and other crimes program to serve 29 western Iowa counties. Through this program, we walk alongside people as they navigate the criminal justice system.

2013

Added a 24/7 statewide hotline to answer calls from people in all 99 Iowa counties.

2015

Added a health clinic advocacy program which places advocates in medical clinics.

2016

Added a Rapid Rehousing program to better serve victims of domestic violence.

2016

Started serving homeless individuals in 10 northwest Iowa counties.

2021

Added family visitation & exchange services to provide supervised visitations and exchanges to have a greater impact on the lives of children.
