Domestic Violence

Resources for Victims and Survivors of Abuse



What is Domestic Violence?

Domestic violence is a pattern of behaviors used to maintain power and control over an intimate partner.

It can happen to anyone, regardless of their race, age, relationship status, sexual orientation, religion or gender.

What Does Abuse Look Like?

Domestic violence can take on numerous forms, but each possesses one commonality: the abuse of power and control. Often, a violent partner will use a combination of these types to maintain control.

Physical Abuse

Physical domestic violence refers to the use of physical force or violence to harm or intimidate an intimate partner or family member. It includes actions such as hitting, punching, kicking, choking, slapping, or any form of physical aggression that causes bodily harm or injury.

Does someone in your life…
  • Destroy your personal property?
  • Deny you access to family assets such as bank accounts, credit cards or car?
  • Control all finances and force you to account for what you spend?
  • Prevent you from working or attending school?
  • Max out credit cards in your name without your permission?
  • Use funds from children’s savings accounts without your permission?

If you answered ‘yes’ to even one of these questions, you might be in an unhealthy or abusive relationship. While this is a list of common behaviors, there could be others that are abusive.

If you have concerns about anything, please call us at 1.800.382.5603

Emotional Abuse

Emotional domestic violence, also known as psychological abuse, involves behaviors that aim to undermine, manipulate, or control the emotions and well-being of a partner or family member. It includes tactics such as constant criticism, humiliation, gaslighting, threats, intimidation, isolating the victim from support networks, and damaging their self-esteem and sense of worth.

Sexual Abuse

Sexual abuse is a violation of a person’s autonomy, dignity, and physical boundaries. It includes acts such as forced sexual intercourse, rape, coerced sexual acts, sexual assault, or any form of sexual coercion, manipulation, or exploitation within an intimate relationship.

Financial Abuse

Financial abuse occurs when the abuser exerts control over the victim’s finances to restrict their independence and undermine their economic well-being. It involves tactics such as controlling or withholding access to money, preventing the victim from working or pursuing education, sabotaging their employment opportunities, exploiting their financial resources, or accumulating debt in their name without consent.
