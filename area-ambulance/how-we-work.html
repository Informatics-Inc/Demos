How We Work

Delivering High-Quality Care to Linn County

Area Ambulance Service is the region’s most trusted transport provider for a reason.

Our lean, self-sufficient model allows us to invest in the best technology and best teams, so we can provide great care without the need for public subsidies. From dynamic deployments to state-of-the-art equipment, we’re working to ensure our patients receive exceptional care when it matters most.

What Makes Us Different

Every detail of our operations is driven by a commitment to faster responses, better outcomes, and exceptional service for our community.

Advanced Ambulances

All 17 of our advanced life support units include cutting-edge lifesaving equipment, from automated CPR machines to remote EKGs. We invest nearly $500,000 in every unit to provide area patients with the best mobile care.

Inside the Ride

Dynamic Deployment

AAS units are strategically posted throughout our entire coverage area by our computer-aided dispatch system, and based on changing call demand requirements. Our teams park at gas stations and retail centers, so they can get to you faster.

Expert Medics

AAS is home to the region’s most experienced and capable paramedics and EMTs. With over 500 combined years of EMS experience, our medics are trained to provide advanced medical care, while acting decisively and compassionately in life’s most critical moments.

How We Respond

Your 911 call for emergency medical services sets a well-defined process into motion:

  • Your call is routed from Linn County’s Communications Division to our 24/6/365 dispatch center.
  • Our dispatchers seamlessly alert the closest ambulance crew to your location.
  • An AAS crew arrives at your location and administers life-saving and stabilizing care.
  • The patient is loaded and the crew travels to the nearest appropriate medical facility.
  • Critical care data (vitals, EKGs, etc.) is transmitted to the hospital en route, so the next care team can take over seamlessly.

Our Team Members

All Area Ambulance Service units are staffed with a minimum of one Paramedic and one EMT at all times, allowing us to provide advanced lifesaving care at all times.

Paramedics

Certified to provide the highest level of pre-hospital care in an EMS setting, including drug administration and EKG review.

EMTs

Certified to provide basic life support skills including CPR, oxygen and more. Each Area Ambulance unit is staffed by a minimum of 1 paramedic and 1 EMT.

Other Team Members

An experienced team of administrative and dispatch professionals dedicated to keeping our ambulance teams in service.

How We Are Funded

Unlike many ambulance services in the U.S., Area Ambulance is funded solely through user fees – we receive absolutely no public funding.

How are we paid?

  • Private insurance (70%)
  • Medicare (20%)
  • Medicaid (10%)
