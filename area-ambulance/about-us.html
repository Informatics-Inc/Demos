About Us

Proudly Serving Linn County Since 1971

Area Ambulance Service (AAS) is the regional 9-1-1 Emergency Medical Services provider for Cedar Rapids, Marion and 15 surrounding communities.

AAS is a community based, not-for-profit organization governed by a board including representatives from St. Luke’s Unity Point Hospital, Mercy Medical Center, the City of Cedar Rapids, the City of Marion, and Linn County. The service is funded solely by user fees, and receives no tax subsidy or funding from any other source.

Services We Provide

Area Ambulance Service is proud to offer a range of transport services and on-site care to Linn County. From emergencies to planned events, our team delivers exceptional care with speed and professionalism.

Emergency Ambulance Service

When seconds matter, our Advanced Life Support ambulances and expert paramedics respond quickly to 9-1-1 calls in Cedar Rapids, Marion, and beyond. Expect skilled pre-hospital care, arriving fast.

Read More

Hospital & Medical Transfers

We are the region’s trusted choice for inter-hospital and facility transfers, as well as for transportation to state medical facilities in Central Iowa. Our fleet of ambulances and rapid response SUVs allow us to serve without compromising local readiness.

Read More

On-Call Event Service

From Corridor festivals to sporting events, our paramedics and EMSs stand ready to serve. Linn County can request on-call service at no cost, as part of our salute to their work.

The Communities We Serve

We serve all of Linn County and beyond from our home base in southwest Cedar Rapids, with a new satellite facility coming to Marion in 2026.

We proudly serve the communities of Bertram, Cedar Rapids, Ely, Fairfax, Marion, Robins, Springville, and Walford.

Financial Assistance

Area Ambulance Service has an established charitable financial assistance program. This program is calculated on a sliding scale based upon family size and annual income. Qualified applicants may receive 10%-100% reduction in their ambulance bill.

To learn more about this program and apply, visit the AAS forms hub.
