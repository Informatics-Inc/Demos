COVID-19

When is there a waiver of the skilled nursing facility (SNF) 100-day benefit period during the COVID-19 public health emergency? For instance, must the beneficiary have COVID-19?
While the beneficiary in question does not need to have COVID-19 for the SNF waiver to apply, the public health emergency must in some way be preventing them from ending their benefit period (i.e. they cannot be discharged for a COVID-related reason). For a more detailed answer, email medicarehelp@shiptacenter.org.
Is the three-day skilled nursing facility (SNF) waiver during the coronavirus public health emergency for all illness or injuries, not just ones that are COVID-related?
Yes. The three-day waiver applies to any reason why someone qualifies for SNF care. They do not have to need SNF care for a COVID-related reason. This Medicare Learning Network (MLN) publication explains the SNF waiver in more detail.
Collapsible Does the skilled nursing facility (SNF) waiver during the coronavirus public health emergency apply to beneficiaries that are on a Medicare Advantage Plan where they only receive 20 days of SNF care at a $0 copay?
Yes. The waiver does not remove cost-sharing, though. It waives the requirement for a three-day hospital stay and it waives the requirement that someone be out of a SNF for 60 days before the benefit period, and therefore covered days, restarts. This MLN explains the SNF waiver in more detail.
