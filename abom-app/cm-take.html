Article Questions:

Characteristics of National Malpractice Claims in Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery

Directions

Diplomates are required to answer 100% of the questions correctly in order to successfully meet the CM component. If a Diplomate does not answer 100% correct, he/she may re-take the assessment or select another article. Please use the reference provided to access a review article and answer the 5 questions that follow. Please be aware that some websites may require personal credentials to login.

Scenario

This is a retrospective study of 2,643 malpractice claims during the period from 2000 to Aug. 2018 using data obtained from the National Practitioner Data Bank. The study focused on information regarding outcome, payments made and duration of lawsuits related to the claims. Also, attention was focused on anesthesia related claims.

Following are the objectives from the references for this Article Questions.

Most claims against OMFS are settled out of court. Anesthesia claims were only 3.6% but averaged higher payout awards compared to all claims. Increasing safety measures such as site and laterality checks prior to procedure could reduce malpractice claims for OMFS. Claims against OMFS were most commonly related to minor permanent injuries occurring in the outpatient setting. The National practitioner Data Bank records all OMFS claims whether settled out of court or adjudicated. Out of court settled claims take less time and result in decreased payments.
