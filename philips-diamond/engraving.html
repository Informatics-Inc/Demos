What is hand engraving?

An age-old art form that uses small steel gravers to cut through metal

Traditionally used to make prints and art; currency plates are hand engraved

The graver is pushed by the palm of the hand; no machinery is used in traditional hand engraving

Highly delicate, fine work that takes many hours to complete

Meet Mitchell Lurth

Mitchell has been an engraver for more than a decade, and a stone setter for five years. He graduated from Emporia State University in Emporia, Kansas with an Engraving Arts Degree (BFA), and completed the GRS Grand Masters Program—a specialized study program for outstanding artists—under Alain Lovenberg, one of the most sought-after hand engravers in the world.

Mitchell’s hand engraving work has appeared everywhere from ornate rings and guns to $35,000 Game of Thrones-branded guitars. He now works as a consultant to GRS, a producer of fine engraving tools, and teaches seminars and clinics around the world.
