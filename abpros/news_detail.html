Board of Prosthodontics announces new certification requirements for practitioners

The Board of Prosthodontics, recently announced new certification requirements that have stirred up controversy in the industry.

Back to News List

The Board of Prosthodontics, a governing body responsible for certifying practitioners in the field of prosthodontics, recently announced new certification requirements that have stirred up controversy in the industry.

The new requirements state that all practitioners seeking certification must now pass an additional series of exams, complete additional clinical training, and submit proof of ongoing professional development. These requirements aim to ensure that practitioners are up-to-date on the latest technologies and techniques in the field, and are able to provide the highest level of care to their patients.

“We understand that change can be difficult,” said a spokesperson for the Board. “But ultimately, we believe that these new requirements will lead to better outcomes for patients and a stronger, more competent prosthodontics industry.”
