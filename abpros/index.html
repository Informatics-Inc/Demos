Understanding Certification

Our Mission

The mission of the American Board of Prosthodontics is to certify individuals who have demonstrated special knowledge and skills in prosthodontics. The board also seeks to certify those who are committed to life-long learning and a lifetime of ethical practices, who value the doctor/patient relationship, who respect those with philosophical, cultural or physical differences and who are committed to the advancement of prosthodontics.

  • Board Certification exams occur twice annually
  • Certifications are good for 8 years

Why should I become
Board Certified

Prosthodontists choose to seek board certification at many different stages of their careers. Some begin the process during their prosthodontic residencies, while others take the exam after many years of experience in academia or private practice.

Four compelling reasons why you should be Board Certified:

  • Validation of expertise
  • Develops specialty expertise
  • Proves that you are up-to-date on the latest advances
  • Demonstrates your commitment to quality care
