Beginning the Planning Process

No one looks forward to preplanning a funeral, but it is an essential part of estate planning.

Preplanning can be very informal, and as simple as jotting down your preferences and sharing your wishes with a family member. More formal arrangements in the form of a preneed contract can also be set up and prefunded through life insurance, a bank trust, or other methods.

We recommend families follow a five-step consideration process as they create their funeral plans:

  1. Communicate Your Wishes. It all starts with your family, so it’s important to communicate what you really want for your funeral. Preplanning takes the pressure off them and removes emotion from the decision-making process.
  2. Choosing a Preplanning Counselor. Work with a counselor who has been trained to serve the best interests of you and your family, preferably as a Certified Preplanning Consultant (CPC).
  3. Consider the Benefits of Prefunding. Prefunding relieves an enormous burden from your loved ones and allows you to lock in today’s prices.
  4. Choose an Established Funeral Home. Look for a partner with a long history of service and a reputation for compassionate care.
  5. Use a Checklist or Preplanning Organizer. Did you know there are 83 things that must be done by the survivor? The right checklist can make all of those decisions easier to track and manage.

Learn more about each of these steps in our blog, “5 Steps to Preplanning Your Funeral,” or contact us today to start planning with one of our certified Family Service Counselors.
